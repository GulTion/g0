An unnamed function taking the input \$(\frac ab,\frac cb)\$ as three arguments in the order b,a,c, and returning a list of four points where the first two points form one segment and the second two points form the other segment. Uses the fact that b must be positive, but doesn't care whether it's the smallest possible value nor whether the fractions are in lowest terms.

The idea is that the "simplest" lattice segment that goes through \$(\frac ab,\frac cb)\$ is the segment from \$(0,0)\$ to \$(a,c)\$. So those are always the first two output points.

Then to get a different segment, just shift the rational point \$1\$ unit to the left, use the same construction, and then shift the segment back to the right. The answer turns out to be the segment from \$(1,0)\$ to \$(a+1-b,c)\$, which are the second two output points.

I hope somebody can use this formula to make a super short entry in some other language...!
