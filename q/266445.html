It outputs in prefix notation, where s is the unary square root operator; all other operators are binary; in this notation, there are only single digits (e.g. 20 represents a 2 then a 0).

It works by repeatedly applying the formula $$ \sin(a+3^{\circ}) = \sin(a) \cos(3^{\circ}) + \cos(a) \sin(3^{\circ}) = \sin(a) \cos(3^{\circ}) + \sqrt{1-\sin^2(a)} \sqrt{1-\cos^2(3^{\circ})}$$

where \$\cos(3^\circ) = \sin(87^\circ)\$ equals $$\frac12 \sqrt{2+\frac12 \sqrt{7+\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{6(5+\sqrt{5})}}}$$
