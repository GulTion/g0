First, Writes an 'a' to STDOUT. Simultaniously it reads a byte from STDIN.

The byte is then tested as a vowel by first confirming:

  • The 2^1 through 2^4 bits are one of the following: 0000, 0100, 0010, 1110, 0101, which is checked via a big "EqualsAny" gate.
  • The 2^0 bit is high through a seperate And gate
  • The 2^5 bit is ignored, as that controls for case.
  • Bits 2^6 and Bits 2^7 are ignores as we are garunteed a letter

When the result is pulled high, an 'n' is sent to STDOUT. Otherwise, the chip terminates.
